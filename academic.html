The Digital Battlefield: Ransomware, Intrusions, and the Road to a Safer System

This study investigates how Swedish companies defend themselves against the growing threat of ransomware. By combining literature research with interviews from six organizations across different industries, the report explores technical preparedness, employee awareness, and the effectiveness of incident response plans.

The results show that most companies maintain a strong level of technical readiness — with updated antivirus systems, network segmentation, and reliable backup strategies. However, the human factor remains the weakest link, making employee training and phishing simulations crucial for reducing risks. Despite high security maturity, many organizations rely heavily on external providers (National Institute of Standards and Technology, 2024), which may hinder internal learning and long-term resilience.

The study also reveals a notable lack of transparency surrounding ransomware incidents. Companies often choose silence over openness, prioritizing reputation protection over collective learning. This secrecy distorts the public perception of cybersecurity threats and slows progress toward a stronger, more resilient digital society.
